# Kazkar.ai — Ideal Customer Profile

## Primary: The Overwhelmed DM
- Runs weekly D&D/Pathfinder/other TTRPG sessions
- Struggles to keep notes during sessions while also running the game
- Wants players to remember what happened last time
- Uses Discord for voice chat
- Tech-comfortable but not a developer
- Pain: "I can't DM and take notes at the same time"

## Secondary: The Lore-Hungry Player
- Plays in 1-3 campaigns
- Wants to remember NPC names, locations, plot threads
- Misses sessions occasionally and needs to catch up
- Pain: "Wait, who was that NPC again?"

## Tertiary: The Actual Play Creator
- Records sessions for community or content
- Wants polished recaps without manual editing
- May share chronicles publicly
- Pain: "Editing session recordings into recaps takes hours"

## Where they hang out online
- Reddit: /r/dnd, /r/DMAcademy, /r/rpg, /r/worldbuilding
- Discord: TTRPG community servers, game-specific servers
- YouTube/Twitch: actual play content
- Forums: RPG.net, EN World, Giant in the Playground

## What they search for
- "how to take notes during d&d"
- "best way to track campaign lore"
- "discord bot for recording d&d"
- "session recap tools"
- "campaign wiki tools"
- "how to remember what happened last session"

## Buying triggers
- Starting a new campaign
- Losing track of NPCs/plot in an existing campaign
- Player asking "what happened last time?"
- DM burnout from note-taking
- Wanting to share campaign stories

## Objections
- "Is it free?" → Free tier with 10 hours
- "Privacy concerns with recording" → Owner controls what's kept, files can auto-delete
- "Will it pick up all speakers?" → Speaker diarization identifies individual voices
- "I already use [X] for notes" → Kazkar is automatic, no manual work during session
