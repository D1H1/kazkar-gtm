# Kazkar.ai — Brand Voice Guide

## Who we are
We're TTRPG players who got tired of forgetting what happened last session. We built Kazkar because we needed it ourselves.

## Tone
- **Casual and warm** — like talking to a friend at the game table
- **Enthusiastic about TTRPG** — we genuinely love this hobby
- **Helpful, not salesy** — share knowledge first, mention product only when relevant
- **Technically capable** — we know Discord, we know audio, we know AI
- **Never condescending** — new players and veteran DMs get equal respect

## Language

### We say
- session, campaign, chronicle, lore, wiki
- DM/GM, player, party, NPC, PC
- summon, banish (our Discord commands)
- "your campaign's story"
- "never miss a moment"

### We don't say
- meeting, project, report, data
- user, customer, subscriber
- "leverage", "synergy", "unlock"
- "AI-powered" (overused, say what it does instead)
- "revolutionary" or "game-changing"

## Reddit voice
- We're a community member first
- Share genuine advice about session management, note-taking, worldbuilding
- Mention Kazkar only when directly relevant to someone's problem
- Never make a post that's just about Kazkar
- Upvote and engage with others' content
- Use TTRPG terminology naturally

## SEO/Blog voice
- Write for DMs and players, not search engines
- Lead with the problem or tip, not the product
- Include practical, actionable advice
- Personal anecdotes from actual play are encouraged
- Product mentions belong in a natural context, not forced CTAs

## Examples

### Good Reddit comment
> I had the same problem — my players constantly forgot NPC names. What helped was keeping a shared Google Doc, but honestly it was hard to update during sessions. I eventually started recording sessions and using the transcripts to build a wiki after. Way less work than taking notes live.

### Bad Reddit comment
> You should try Kazkar.ai! It records your sessions and builds a wiki automatically! Check it out at kazkar.ai!

### Good blog intro
> Every DM knows the feeling: you've spent hours prepping an intricate political web of NPCs, and your players can't remember the name of the quest-giver from last week. Here are five ways to help your party keep track of campaign lore.

### Bad blog intro
> Kazkar.ai is an AI-powered session recording platform that revolutionizes how TTRPG groups manage their campaign lore.
